About

Hi, I'm Guillaume Nadal, I recently completed my Master’s degree in Engineering Physics at Polytechnique Montréal, where I conducted my thesis research in the Nano Quantum Semiconductor Laboratory. My work bridged experimental solid-state physics, quantum systems, and data analysis. My Projects

Current :

APT reconstruction

Atom Probe Tomography (APT) is an advanced microscopy technique that employs field ion emission to extract individual atoms from a material, enabling a precise 3D reconstruction of its atomic structure. This project aims to enhance reconstruction accuracy by integrating crystal plane data.

Cadmium Zinc Telluride

This project focuses on understanding the defect structures in the CdZnTe alloy, a material widely used in medical imaging for X-ray detection. CdZnTe crystals often contain various structural defects, and gaining insight into these surrounding defects can directly contribute to improving the crystal growth process.

Sample management interface

Developed a full-stack interface to manage diverse samples and securely store experimental data for our research group. Leveraging Next.js for the frontend, Django for the API, and PostgreSQL for data storage, this project streamlines our workflow, prevents data loss, and ensures clear tracking of each step in the microfabrication process.

Past :

Quantum Computing Simulation

Quandela is a company focused on developing optical quantum computing using single-photon sources. Perceval, their simulation library, models the behavior of photons as they move through various gates and optical paths. I made simulations to analyze how delays in photon emission impact the fidelity of the output.

Braking system design

I was a member of the technical society at Polyloop, where I designed a braking system of the HyperLoop capsule. I used SolidWorks to make a design.
